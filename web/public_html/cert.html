Certificate generation

Guide

We'll guide you through the creation of your own keypair, for use with OpenVPN.

Please make sure you understand the following:
Under no circumstance, you are to copy or move your private key off the system it has been generated on. You are also required to make sure the private is kept safely and securely, is only accessible by you and cannot be read or altered without your knowledge. Neutrinet cannot be held liable for VPN clients, so please take care.

When you think your key has been compromised or you need a key for another OpenVPN instance, you can always go to your account page and easily rekey. You can choose to retract a public key at any time, we will take care of the rest.

With that said, let's go ahead and create your keypair and CSR:

# Windows users may need to download OpenSSL first.
# enter the following commands in a terminal or command prompt # navigate to directory in which you want your keys
# remember, this should be a private directory
# preferably in your home folder and not shared via Dropbox etc.
cd [directory that will hold your keys]

# generate your keypair and the certificate signing request
# take care to certainly include a Common Name (CN) in your request, other fields are
# optional yet useful openssl req -out CSR.csr -new -newkey rsa:4096 -nodes -keyout client.key

cat CSR.csr # paste the contents below

Neutrinet CA handling

Neutrinet's CA is secured as good as possible, but due to bugs in the control stack, OpenVPN or humans, we may require you to rekey whenever a problem occurs. We will prompt you via email and communicate across all our public channels, should you not react we will try other means of communication, with clear and simple instructions on how to rekey (which will mostly involve download a script and/or program which executes the OpenSSL commands for you and automatically rekeys with our management service).

Do not modify your keypair at the request of third parties.
